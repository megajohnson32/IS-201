History of The Paper Clip


The paper clip was first introduced by a Norgewian inventor in the late 19th century. This invention helped greatly during the "paper explosion" of the Industrial Revolution. Over the years, the paper clip has been reformed into many different shapes and sizes before settling on the design we have today as the most effective shape and style. With it's first use being to store and bind pages together without puncturing the paper, this tool can now be used in many different way. For example, during World War II, wearing a paper clip on your uniform served as a silent badge of resistance for Norwegians opposing Nazi occupation. Since the paper clip invention, these small clips now symbolize many different things, including:

  1. Unity and Resistance

    • Most famous and symbolic use during WWII

  2. "Perfect" Design and Minimalism

    • The ultimate symbol of efficiency in the world of engineering and architecture

  3. Connection and Memory

    • A universal icon for email attachments, symbolizing connection between different pieces of information



It is now estimated that only 10% of paper clips are actually used for papers. People are still discovering purposes and uses for these office tools. Paper clips appear to be more useful than most people think. Some of these uses include, but are not limited to:

  • Makeshift lock picks
  • Tech-reset tools
  • Bookmarks
  • Jewelry
  • Silent Figet Tools
  • Sim Card Remover
  • Makeshift Zipper Pull
  • Organizing Cables
